City design is a complex and multifaceted challenge that requires careful planning and optimization. The City Design Project aims to explore various algorithmic approaches to solve urban planning problems such as optimal road network design, facility placement, traffic flow optimization, and resource allocation.

In this project, we will utilize Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA) techniques to address real-world city planning scenarios. Our approach will involve implementing graph algorithms, dynamic programming solutions, greedy algorithms, and optimization techniques to create efficient and sustainable city layouts.

The project focuses on practical applications such as minimizing travel distances, optimizing public transportation routes, ensuring efficient emergency response systems, and planning green spaces. Through computational analysis and algorithmic thinking, we aim to demonstrate how computer science can contribute to better urban environments.
