üåç Problem Context

Rajdwar represents a growing urban region that must balance economic development, liveability, sustainability, and accessibility. Every design decision ‚Äî where to place hospitals, markets, industries, roads, and transport hubs ‚Äî affects travel time, pollution, safety, and quality of life.

Rather than treating this as just ‚Äúdrawing a map‚Äù, we treat it as a computational problem. Nodes, edges, weights, constraints, and objectives together define an algorithmic view of the city.

üéØ Design Goals

  • Minimize average travel time between key locations.
  • Ensure every residential cluster has access to health, education, and markets within a bounded radius.
  • Optimize placement of high-impact structures like factories, hospitals, IT parks, and public transport.
  • Align the design with SDG-11 and related SDGs for sustainable, inclusive urban development.

üß† DAA Concepts

Our city layout uses several core ideas from design and analysis of algorithms:

  • Graphs: Intersections, zones, and key structures are nodes; roads and paths are edges.
  • Dijkstra‚Äôs Algorithm: Used conceptually for shortest-path travel between important locations.
  • Greedy Choices: Selecting locations for critical services (like hospitals) to cover maximum population first.
  • Clustering / Partitioning: Dividing the city into sectors (east, west, industrial, commercial, etc.) assigned to each team member.
  • Complexity Thinking: Understanding trade-offs between rich connectivity (more edges) and cost / congestion.
